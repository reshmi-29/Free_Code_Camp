A Tribute to the Last Messenger of Allah, Prophet Muhammad (SAW)

Biography

Hazrat Muhammad (SAW) was born in 570 CE in Mecca, in present-day Saudi Arabia. He belonged to the Quraysh tribe, a respected tribe known for its leadership in the region.

  • Orphaned at a young age, raised by his grandfather and uncle.
  • Known for his honesty and integrity, earning the nickname "Al-Amin" (the Trustworthy).

Prophethood

At the age of 40, while meditating in the Cave of Hira, he received his first revelation from the Angel Gabriel (Jibril).

  • Marked the beginning of his prophethood and a new chapter in human history.
  • Received revelations that formed the basis of the Quran over the next 23 years.
  • His prophethood began in 610 CE and continued until his death in 632 CE.

Teachings

Prophet Muhammad (SAW) preached monotheism, emphasizing the oneness of God (Allah).

  • Encouraged compassion, justice, and equality.
  • Stressed the importance of prayer (Salah), charity (Zakat), and good conduct (Akhlak).
  • Urged his followers to treat others with respect and kindness, regardless of their background.

Significant Events

One of the most significant events in his life was the Hijra, the migration from Mecca to Medina in 622 CE.

  • Marked the beginning of the Islamic calendar.
  • Established the first Muslim community in Medina.
  • Built a society based on Islamic principles in Medina, acting as both a spiritual leader and a political figure.
  • The Treaty of Hudaybiyyah in 628 CE facilitated the spread of Islam.

Opposition and Persecution

Muhammad's early teachings faced strong opposition from the wealthy Quraysh clan.

  • Initially faced ridicule and sarcasm, followed by active persecution.
  • Directed some followers to migrate to Abyssinia (Ethiopia) to escape persecution.

Last Years in Mecca

Following the death of his uncle Abu Talib, Muhammad was exposed to greater hostility.

  • Faced severe hostility during his visit to Ta'if, where he was pelted with stones.
  • Met with tribes from Yathrib (Medina), leading to the pledges of allegiance known as the "First" and "Second Pledges of al-Aqaba".

Emigration to Medina

Due to threats from the Quraysh, Muhammad emigrated to Medina in 622 CE.

  • Established the first mosque in Medina and the Constitution of Medina.
  • Set the foundation for a multi-religious society.

Battles and Expansion

The Battle of Badr in 624 CE marked a significant confrontation between Muslims and Meccan pagans.

  • Considered a miraculous victory for Muslims, with divine support.
  • After the battle, Muhammad received the Sword Zulfiqar from the archangel Gabriel.

Legacy

The impact of his teachings is profound. After his passing in 632 CE, Islam rapidly spread across the Arabian Peninsula and beyond.

  • One of the world's major religions, followed by over a billion people today.
  • His life and teachings continue to guide Muslims globally.
  • The compilation of his sayings and actions, known as Hadith, enriches Islamic teachings.

Conclusion

Prophet Muhammad (SAW) remains a symbol of peace and mercy.

  • His legacy as the final messenger of Allah inspires countless individuals.
  • Exemplifies patience, humility, and service to humanity.
